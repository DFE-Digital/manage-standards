Standards move through several stages before they are published.

  1. Propose and draft a standard

    A new or revised standard is proposed and drafted in a Word document, using a template. Using the template will help you detail the identified need, scope, and objective of the standard.

    It's then discussed and reviewed whether to take it forward by the Head of Profession or profession-specific working groups.

  2. Submit for standard forum review

    {#

    Use Manage DDT standards to submit the proposed standard to the standards forum.

    #}

    Send an email with a link or copy of your draft standard, to the standards inbox for the standards forum for review. They will contact you with next steps.

    The forum consists of subject matter experts and key stakeholders who evaluate the standard for accuracy, feasibility, and alignment with department approach to standards.

    A decision is made by the forum who can approve, reject, or ask for amendments to the standard.

    In January, there will be a new digital product to submit and manage the standard.

  3. Outcome

    The standards forum will provide feedback to the standard owner and submittor.

    Approval

    The working group approves the standard.

    At this point, comms and other processes are put in place before final publication.

    Rejection

    If the standard fails to meet criteria during the review, it is rejected.

    The reasons for rejection are provided back to the owner, and the standard may either be abandoned or revised for resubmission.

  4. Published

    Once approved, the standard can be published and made widely available in the Standards manual.

  5. Archived

    Standards no longer required or abandoned, may be unpublished for an indeterminable amount of time and either revised or republished later.

    Standard could be superseded by another standard.
