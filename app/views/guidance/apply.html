Standards exist to ensure we deliver high-quality, secure, and accessible digital, data, and technology (DDT) products and services. Applying the right standards at the right time helps to:

  • build consistent and reliable services
  • reduce risks and improve compliance
  • meet user needs effectively

This page explains how to apply DDT standards in your role, with examples to make it clearer.

Find and review the standards

Use the Standards manual to find the latest version of the standards. Each standard includes:

  • the purpose and need for the standard
  • how to meet the standard
  • any applicable products or services to help meet or apply it
  • additional guidance and tools to support understanding the standard

You can also see the history of changes to the standard, to understand how it has evolved over time.

The standard published in the manual will always be the latest published version of the standard.

Understand the standards relevant to your role

Different roles interact with standards in different ways. Start by identifying the standards that apply to your work.

You can filter standards by categories, to help you find standards appropriate for your role or profession.

Some examples:

Designers

Focus on accessibility and user-centred design standards to ensure your work is inclusive and meets user needs, branding and style guides.

Developers

Look at technical and security standards, such as approved coding practices and data handling requirements.

User researchers

Read about how to carry out user research in a safe way, handing participant data and planning research activity.

Review and iterate

Applying standards isn’t a one-off task. Make sure you regularly review your work against standards and check that your project remains compliant as it evolves.

Standards may change over time – stay informed by reviewing the Standards Manual regularly and use the standard history against all standards, to check for any changes.

If you’re unsure about how to apply a standard, speak to the standard owner listed on the standard.

Get help and feedback

If you’re unsure about how to apply a standard you can:

  • speak to the standard owner listed on the standard
  • if you’re in a profession with a Head of Profession, speak to them for profession-specific guidance
  • join a community of practice to learn from colleagues in similar roles
  • contact the standards forum or use the standards slack channel for more support

If you have suggestions for improvements to the Standards Manual or other products in the standards service, use the feedback feature on the bottom of all pages.
